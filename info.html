When And Where Was The Great Emu War

  • The Great Emu War was in 1932 Australia was limping through the great depression, which impacted everyone.
  • It was after World War I, so everyone except the US was recovering from the war
  • The war was of course in Australia.

How Did The Great Emu War Start

  • A horde of 20,000 emus were trampling fences and feeding on wheat (one of their favorite foods) while heading over to an area with water.
  • Farmers couldn’t defend their farms against the emu horde.
  • Authorities called in the army.
  • Defence Minister George Pearce agreed to send Lewis machine guns with soldiers to use them against the emus.

What Are Some Solutions That The Austalians Took to Stop The Emus

  • The Australians took the obvious route, population control, or killing the emus with lewis guns.
  • They killed a few emus before their guns jammed and the emus scattered
  • A week and a half later they opened fire again killing about 1000 emus, ~10 rounds a kill

Conclusion of the War

  • The Emu War resulted in the emus winning, “With the emus smarter -and faster- than the soldiers had anticipated”
  • “If we had a military division with the bullet-carrying capacity of these birds, it would face any army in the world. They could face machine guns with the invulnerability of tanks.” says Major G.P.W. Meredith.
  • The Australians couldn’t attack the emus anymore because the federal government refused to let them, the Australians lost.
